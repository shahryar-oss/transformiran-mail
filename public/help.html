🧭 The basics

How the app is laid out, so everything else makes sense.

Transform Iran Mail is your normal Gmail mailbox with a smarter face on top. You sign in with your Transform Iran Google account, and everything you do here (send, archive, label) happens in your real Gmail too.

The three columns

  • Left rail — your navigation: the + New email button, your folders, To Do, Promises, your Important people, your real Gmail labels, and links to Calendar, Contacts and Settings.
  • Middle column — the email list, with a search bar and colour-coded filter pills.
  • Right side — the email you're reading, a mini-calendar, and the Delta chat panel.

The other pages

Across the top of the rail and via keyboard shortcuts you can reach five dedicated pages: To Do, Calendar, Contacts, Promises and Settings. Each is covered below.

First time? The very first sign-in shows a short welcome screen, and Delta quietly reads your inbox history in the background so it can search your past mail. A progress bar at the top of the inbox shows how that's going — you can keep working while it runs.

📥 The Inbox

Your main screen — read, triage and act on mail.

Open the inbox →

The left rail, top to bottom

  • + New email — opens the writing window (see Writing a new email).
  • Inbox, Starred, Sent, Drafts, Archive, Trash — your standard folders, each with a live count.
  • Snoozed — mail you've set aside to return later (see Snooze).
  • Marketing and Done — newsletters, and mail you've finished with.
  • To Do and Promises — your tasks and the commitments you've made (these sit near the top so they're easy to reach).
  • Important — your hand-picked VIPs (see Important people).
  • Folders — your real Gmail labels, pulled straight from your account. Click any to filter.
  • Calendar, Contacts, Settings — links to those pages. Your name and a sign-out arrow sit at the very bottom.

The email list

Each row shows the sender, time, subject, a one-line preview, a 📎 icon if there are attachments, and a colour-coded category tag. The list loads more as you scroll (no paging).

Category tags & filter pills

Delta reads every email and labels it so you can see what matters at a glance. Click a pill at the top of the list to show only that kind:

TagMeans
UrgentTime-sensitive and aimed at you — handle today.
ReplySomeone is waiting on your answer.
TaskContains an action item for you.
InternalFrom the team, no urgent ask.
Receipt / FYI / Newsletter / AutoInvoices, things you're just cc'd on, bulk mail, and automated alerts.
DoneYou've replied and finished — archived with a green tick.
Smart, not literal Delta reads the whole email, not just the snippet. If you're only in Cc (not To), it leans toward FYI — unless the body actually asks you something. Mail from your Important people gets a nudge up in priority.

Use the refresh button to pull the newest mail, and the re-classify button (circular arrow) to re-run Delta's tagging if you've just updated how it sorts things.

Quick actions on hover

Hover any row to reveal one-click buttons: mark read/unread, star, archive, and delete. No need to open the email first.

The 🔔 bell at the top of the list shows overdue promises, due tasks, and unread mail from Important people — click it for the list.

✉️ Reading & replying

Open an email and the right-hand reader shows it in full.

The reader toolbar

Reply, Reply All, Forward, Archive, Snooze, Star, mark unread, and Delete. Attachments appear as chips — click to preview a PDF, image or document right inside the app, or ⌘-click to open in a new tab.

Reply All keeps everyone When you Reply All, the people in Cc are kept by default — so nobody silently drops off the thread. You can still remove anyone before sending.

Let Delta help with the email in front of you

Under the email you'll find one-tap Delta buttons:

Draft a replyDelta writes a reply in your voice. It opens ready to edit — nothing sends until you click Send.
SummarizeA three-bullet summary of a long email.
TranslateTurns Farsi, Armenian, Dutch and other languages into English.
Ask about thisAsk Delta a question about the email's content.
Add to To DoPulls out the action items and turns them into tasks.

The reply window

When Delta drafts a reply you get an editable draft plus quick tone buttons — Match my style, Shorter, Warmer, More formal, Firmer, Apologetic, In Farsi, In Dutch — or type your own instruction (e.g. "mention the flight is at 4:55 PM") and Delta re-drafts. A banner tells you how confident it is (how many past emails to that person it matched your style on), and a "Delta consulted N sources" panel lists exactly what it read to write the draft.

It learns your voice The edits you make to drafts teach Delta how you write. Over time, replies come back closer to how you'd say it yourself.

Send safely

After you click Send, a short Undo countdown appears (you set the length in Settings). If the email you're replying to was linked to a task, that task ticks itself off automatically.

📝 Writing a new email

Click + New email in the rail (or press c) to open the writing window.

  • To / Cc / Bcc — start typing a name or address and it suggests contacts. Cc and Bcc are hidden until you click them.
  • Formatting — font, size, colour, bold/italic/underline, lists, links, quotes — a full rich-text toolbar.
  • Signature — your Gmail signature can be inserted automatically; control this in Settings.
  • Save as draft — keeps it in Gmail Drafts to finish later.
  • Right-to-left aware — Farsi and Arabic text automatically align correctly.
Reply in their language If you reply to a Farsi email but type in English, a chip offers to translate your message to Farsi before sending — the quoted history is left untouched.

⭐ Important people

Your own VIP list — nobody is added for you.

The Important section in the rail is yours to build. Each person becomes a folder; clicking it shows only the emails they sent you over the past year — not threads where they're merely cc'd. That keeps it to the conversations that are genuinely about them.

To add someone

  • Click the + next to "Important" and enter their email, or
  • Open one of their emails and click Important next to their name.

To remove someone

Click the × next to their name in the rail.

Why it matters Mail from your Important people is nudged up in priority and feeds the 🔔 notifications and the morning brief — so the people you care about don't get lost.

🗂️ Folders & labels

Your real Gmail structure, mirrored here.

Alongside the standard folders (Inbox, Starred, Sent, Drafts, Archive, Trash), the Folders section lists your actual Gmail labels. Make a label in Gmail and it shows up here; click any folder to filter the list to it. Archiving here removes mail from the inbox but keeps it in Gmail, so it's always searchable.

🔍 Search

Find anything across your whole mailbox.

Type in the search bar (or press /) and press Enter. It understands Gmail-style search:

  • from:lana — everything from Lana
  • subject:budget — by subject word
  • newer_than:1y — recent only
  • Combine them: from:simon subject:wire

Press Esc to clear. You can also ask Delta to search in plain English ("find Lana's emails about the budget") — and clicking an email in Delta's results filters the list to just those, with a banner you can clear.

⏰ Snooze

Hide an email now, have it come back later.

Open an email and click Snooze (or press b). Pick Later today, Tomorrow morning, Tomorrow evening, In 2 days, Next week, In a week, or choose a custom date and time. The email leaves your inbox immediately and reappears, marked unread, at the time you chose. Everything snoozed sits in the Snoozed folder until then.

Set your own defaults Change the preset snooze times in Settings → Inbox.

✅ To Do (tasks)

A full task manager, in the style of Microsoft To Do.

Open To Do →

Lists down the left

  • My Day — what you're doing today.
  • Important — anything you've starred.
  • Planned — a calendar-style view grouped into Today, Tomorrow, This week and Later, so you can see what's coming. Overdue items are flagged in red.
  • All and Completed — everything, and everything done.
  • Your own lists — make as many as you like with + New list.

Working with a task

Type in + Add a task to create one. Click a task to open its detail panel, where you can set:

  • Due date — when it's due.
  • Reminder — pick "5 minutes from now", "Tomorrow 9 AM", "Next Monday", or a custom time. You'll get a browser notification at that moment. If notifications aren't on yet, a "Turn on reminders" chip in the header switches them on.
  • Steps — break a task into sub-steps with their own checkboxes.
  • Notes — free text.
  • Source email — if the task came from an email, the original is shown right there; click to jump back to it.
Hands-free Tell Delta "remind me to call Reza on Friday" (typed or by voice) and it creates the task for you.

📅 Calendar

Your Google Calendar, in a clean month grid.

Open Calendar →
  • Month grid with a mini-month picker to jump dates, plus a "Today & upcoming" agenda on the right.
  • Create an event — click + New event, or double-click a day for a quick add. Set title, time, all-day, which calendar, location, attendees and a description.
  • Multiple calendars — toggle each Google calendar on or off.
  • Event details — click any event to see time, location, attendees (with who's accepted), and a button to open it in Google Calendar.
  • Default meeting link — set your usual video link in Settings and it's attached to new meetings automatically.
Delta schedules too Ask Delta to "find a time when Pia and Lana are both free" and it checks everyone's calendar, then proposes a slot for you to confirm. It respects your focus blocks and the gap you like between meetings — and it never invents a meeting link (see Honesty & accuracy).

👥 Contacts

Everyone you email, gathered automatically.

Open Contacts →
  • Auto-built — contacts are gathered from your mail; the Refresh from inbox button rescans for new senders.
  • Search & sort — by name, A–Z, recently active, or most contacted.
  • Each contact shows email, phone, organisation, job title and notes (all editable), plus their recent emails — click one to jump to it.
  • Quick actions — email the person, mark them Important, or add a new contact by hand.

🤝 Promises

The commitments you make in email, tracked so you don't drop them.

Open Promises →

When you send an email and say something like "I'll send you the report by Friday," Delta notices and records it as a promise, with the deadline you mentioned. You don't have to do anything.

  • Open / Overdue / Fulfilled / Cancelled — filter by status. Overdue promises show in red and feed your 🔔 notifications.
  • Auto-closes — when the other person replies in the thread, the promise is marked fulfilled.
  • Mark done / Dismiss — close one yourself, or dismiss it if Delta picked up something that wasn't really a commitment.
  • Each promise links back to the original email it came from.
Only real promises Delta tracks concrete commitments ("I'll send X by Friday"), not vague pleasantries ("let me know if you need anything").

🔺 Meet Delta

Your built-in assistant — it reads your inbox so it can help you reply faster and stay on top of everything.

Open Delta with the logo button at the bottom-right of any screen. You can type or use your voice. Delta can read your mail, search your history, draft replies, manage tasks and your calendar, dig into attachments, and answer questions — always showing its work and never sending anything without you.

How to talk to it

Just say what you want, the way you'd ask a person:

"What's urgent today?" · "Draft a warm reply to Pia about the Tehran trip." · "Find Simon's last email about the wire." · "Add a task to review the budget by Friday." · "Summarise the PDF Pia attached."

The welcome screen also offers one-tap starters: Organize my inbox Quick sweep Find urgent emails Plan my day Who am I behind on? Summarize inbox Translate non-English mail

Save your favourites Frequently used instructions can be saved as reusable prompts (the bookmark button by the chat box, or Settings → Memory) and run any time, in text or voice.

🎙️ Voice mode

Talk to Delta out loud and it talks back — a real two-way conversation.

Click the voice button in the Delta panel header and allow your microphone. An animated orb shows when Delta is listening, thinking and speaking, and your words appear as you speak.

  • Everything you can do by typing, you can do by voice: draft replies, create tasks and reminders, search your inbox, schedule meetings.
  • It understands and speaks several languages, including Farsi, English and Dutch.
  • Anything Delta creates during the call (like a draft) appears in the app afterwards, ready for you. The full transcript is saved to the chat, and you can copy it.
  • End the session by saying goodbye or tapping the End button.

🧠 Memory & learning

Delta gets more useful the longer it works with you.

  • You can tell it to remember — say "remember that Pia handles Tehran logistics" and it keeps that for later.
  • It notices patterns — quietly, overnight, it may learn things like "replies in Farsi to Lazarus" and suggest saving them. You'll always see what it saved, with an undo.
  • It learns your writing — your edits to drafts shape how it writes for you.
  • You're in control — view, edit or delete everything Delta remembers in Settings → Memory.

💬 Slack & voice notes

Connect Slack (in Settings) and Delta can search it alongside your email.

Ask things like "what did Anet say about the budget?" or "summarise my conversation with Simon." Delta searches channels, direct messages and threads.

Voice notes

Delta automatically transcribes Slack voice clips so you can search and ask about them — even ones recorded long ago. Ask "what did Anet and I talk about?" and it pulls the voice notes from that conversation and explains them.

Farsi voice notes Delta transcribes Farsi voice notes and translates them to English, but flags them as "auto-transcribed, may be imperfect." Use them for the gist, not for exact wording.

🌅 Morning brief

A daily head-start, ready the first time you open Delta each day.

Delta pulls together your overnight email, today's meetings, open tasks and any due promises into a short brief: a one-line headline, your top priorities, and a queue of pre-written replies for the most urgent emails. Dismiss one and the next slides in — send with a click, or open to edit first.

🛡️ Honesty & accuracy

How Delta avoids making things up — and what to expect.

Like all AI assistants, Delta could in theory state something that sounds right but isn't. We've built in firm rules to prevent that with your data:

  • Facts must come from somewhere real — Delta will only state a specific fact (a date, an amount, a name, a grant) if it found it in the email you're reading, in something it searched for this turn, or in its saved memory. It won't invent details to fill a gap.
  • If it can't find it, it says so — you'll get "I couldn't find that, let me search" rather than a confident guess. That's deliberate: a slower honest answer beats a fast wrong one.
  • It never invents a meeting link — for video calls it only uses your saved default link, or one you paste into the chat. It will never make up a Zoom URL.
  • Drafts show their sources — the "Delta consulted N sources" panel lists every email and attachment a draft was based on, so you can check it.
Best practice For anything that really matters — a figure, a commitment, a name — glance at the source Delta cites, or ask it to "show me the email." It's built to make that easy.

⚙️ Settings

Make the app yours.

Open Settings →

What you can change

  • Account — your profile and sign-out.
  • Inbox — your Important contacts and your default snooze times.
  • Compose — the Undo send delay (off, 5/10/20/30s), whether Markdown shortcuts turn into formatting on send, your aliases and signature (on every message, first message only, or never), and reusable snippets of text.
  • Calendar — your default meeting link, the buffer between meetings, and focus blocks (protected time Delta won't book over).
  • Appearance — Light / Dark / System theme, an accent colour, and text density & font.
  • Integrations — connect or disconnect Slack.
  • Memory — review, edit and delete everything Delta remembers, and manage your saved prompts.
  • Keyboard shortcuts — remap any shortcut to keys you prefer.
  • Notifications — see below.
Privacy by default Transform Iran Mail does not track whether the people you email open your messages or click your links. That's a deliberate choice, not a setting.

🔔 Notifications

Stay informed without being buried.

  • Turn on browser notifications, then choose what alerts you: new mail, task reminders, calendar alerts.
  • Quiet hours — set a window (and days) when nothing interrupts you.
  • Hourly budget — cap how many alerts can fire in any hour.
  • Per-contact — always notify for some people, mute others entirely.
  • In the inbox, the 🔔 bell gathers overdue promises, due tasks and unread mail from your Important people.

🗝️ Admin console

For administrators only — an organisation-wide view of how the app is used. Most people will never need this.

The admin console lives at /admin and has its own passphrase login, separate from your mailbox sign-in. It's intended for the operator (Shahryar).

Overview & users

  • Headline numbers — total and active users, emails handled, Delta calls, and Delta's running cost — over the last 7, 30 or 90 days.
  • Delta cost over time — a chart of daily spend.
  • Usage by model — how much each level (Basic/Advanced/Expert) is used and what it costs.
  • Users table — per person: last active, time in the app, emails, Delta calls, cost, and status. Click a row for a detail panel with their recent Delta chats.
  • Block / unblock — an administrator can block a user, which immediately ends their access.

Delta chat monitoring

The most recent Delta conversations across everyone, newest first — used to spot-check that Delta's tone, accuracy and refusals are right. This is a quality-control tool for the administrator.

🔒 Privacy & safety

  • Your mailbox is yours. Delta reads your inbox to help you, and one person's data is never shared with another.
  • No read-tracking. The app doesn't tell you (or anyone) when recipients open your email or click links.
  • Delta can't act without you. It never sends, deletes or moves money on its own.
  • Keep secrets out of chat. Enter passwords and sensitive credentials in their proper settings, not in the chat box.
  • Works alongside your team. For judgement calls — complex travel, sensitive decisions — loop in your human assistant. Delta is the fast digital layer, not a replacement for people.

💡 Tips & FAQ

The page feels slow the first time

The very first load fetches the app's files; after that they're cached and pages switch almost instantly. If something looks stale after an update, a hard refresh (⌘+Shift+R) clears it.

Delta says it can't find an email I know exists

If you've just joined, Delta may still be indexing your older mail (watch the progress bar at the top of the inbox). Try again shortly, or ask it to search again — and remember it would rather say "I couldn't find it" than guess.

A draft has a detail I didn't expect

Open the "Delta consulted N sources" panel to see exactly what it read. If a fact isn't traceable there, treat it as a draft suggestion and confirm it.

Can I use this on my phone?

Yes — the layout adapts to smaller screens. Voice mode works wherever your browser can use the microphone.

Which language will Delta reply in?

It matches you. Write to it in Farsi and it answers in Farsi; the same goes for drafting replies to people who write in another language.

Something's wrong or missing

Tell your administrator — they can see usage and help, and the team ships fixes quickly.
